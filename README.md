## **Educational Purpose**

### **Knowledge**
* **Latest Developments:** Possesses relevant knowledge about the latest developments and challenges in the field of artificial intelligence (AI) security.
* **General Theories:** Possesses an up-to-date knowledge of the relevant general theories in AI security, as well as their connections with other professional and/or scientific domains.
* **Terminology:** Is a skilful user of the relevant scientific terminology in English.

### **Abilities**
* **Problem Formalization:** Is capable of formalizing and describing complex problems in the field of AI security.
* **Solution Design:** Is capable of designing, developing, and maintaining data security solutions and environments.
* **Value Assessment:** Is capable of assessing the business and innovative value of data security solutions, as well as validating and conveying their use in different industry domains.
* **Requirements Mapping:** Is capable of mapping customer requirements to appropriate solutions in the data security domain.

### **Attitude**
* **Stays Current:** Follows the latest professional and scientific developments in relevant domains.
* **Critical Evaluation:** Is devoted to retrospective analytics and evaluation of past performance.
* **Lifelong Learning:** Is devoted to lifelong learning and acquiring novel competencies in related fields.

### **Autonomy & Responsibility**
* **Professional Decisions:** Takes full responsibility for his/her professional decisions in the field of AI security.

## **Practical Skills & Outcomes**
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1. **Understand** fundamental concepts of machine-learning security and privacy.
2. **Implement** state-of-the-art attacks (Evasion, Poisoning, Inversion) in Python.
3. **Evaluate** model robustness using quantitative metrics and certified bounds.
4. **Design** multi-layered defense strategies (DP, FL, Robust Training) for production systems.
5. **Generate** privacy-preserving synthetic data for sensitive domains.
